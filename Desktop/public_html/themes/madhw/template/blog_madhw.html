Cryptocurrency – pros and cons. Should it be legalized in India?
Cryptocurrency is a digital payment system that doesn't rely on banks to verify transactions. It is secured by cryptography, which makes it nearly impossible to counterfeit or double-spend. Many cryptocurrencies are decentralized networks based on blockchain technology—a distributed ledger enforced by a disparate network of computers. A defining feature of cryptocurrencies is that they are generally not issued by any central authority, rendering them theoretically immune to government interference or manipulation.
    Advantages of Cryptocurrencies:-

  1. 1: Incredible Security: Most of the cryptocurrencies are based on blockchain technology. It allows online secure payments, i.e., digitally known to be tokens.

  2. 2: Short Settlement Time and Low Fees: You can transfer any amount from one wallet to another anywhere in the world in less than 10 min

  3. 3: More Private Transactions: It is p2p, the transaction information isn't disclosed to any third party.

  4. 4: Portfolio Diversification: You can invest in over 1200+ listed cryptos to diversify your risk on investment.

  5. 5: Cross-Border Payments: International transactions have no extra fees and take no extra time.

  6. 6: 24/7 Markets: Unlike the stock market, Cryptocurrencies markets stay over every time, day or night.

  7. 7: Potential for high returns: Crypto's can grow over 1000% in a single day.


    8. Disadvantages of Cryptocurrencies:-

  8. 1: Wallets Can Be Lost: There is no third-party interference, forgotten passkey can't be recovered.

  9. 2: No Physical Form: cryptocurrency is completely digital and there is no physical form of it.

  10. 3: High volatility and potential for large losses: Crypto's can fall over 100% in a single day.

  11. 4: Poor store of value and limited acceptance: Currently, we can only exchange cryptocurrency through third party exchanges. Unfortunately, we can't use it on daily life basis.

From ban to regulation, the cryptocurrency’s a journey so far in India: In 2013, the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) issued a circular warning to the public against the use of virtual currencies. The bank warned users, holders, and traders of virtual currencies about the potential financial, operational, legal, customer protection, and security-related risks they are exposing themselves to. Currently, there is no regulation or any ban on the use of cryptocurrencies in the country. The Reserve Bank of India’s (RBI) order banning banks from supporting crypto transactions, was reversed by the Supreme Court order of March 2020.

Conclusion: In my opinion, Cryptocurrency should not be banned as it is decentralized. The only solution is to regulate trading and staking. The banning will only result in leaving India as a backward Economy.
